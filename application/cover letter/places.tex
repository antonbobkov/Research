%of the schools in Groups I, II and III of the AMS survey rankings.
%Not all of these places have posted job openings; if they have not, I
%have determined the department address instead.
%
%Job information is listed as follows:
%0. The name of the school followed by its AMS grouping.
%1. The first line before any entry describes what sort of posting the
%   institution made.  NO POSTING means just that.  OLD POSTING means that
%   I found a listing for pre-2000 jobs, but none for this year.
%   CONFIRMED means that they posted a job opening.
%2. Next comes the address for use by super.tex and labels.tex.
%3. Next is the title of the job, if known.
%4. Next is the web address of the math department, or even of the
%   job posting if it is posted on the department web page.
%   If there is more information known, it is arranged as follows:
%5. Deadline : Obvious meaning.  (soft) indicates that review of applications
%   begins then, but they continue to accept them afterwards.
%6. Forms : This describes the various forms the department requires.
%   Mostly self-explanatory.  ``Research'' indicates that they want
%   a research summary/plan.  ``AMS'' refers to the AMS standard cover
%   sheet.  ``local'' means that the department has some form of their
%   own, which is usually available at the web address listed previously.
%7. Posted : Where the job has been posted.  ``local'' means the department
%   web page.  ``AMS'' means the AMS on-line job posting site.  ``AMS8/9''
%   means the 8th or 9th volume of the 1999 Notices.  ``mail'' means that
%   they mailed an advertisement to Harvard.
%8. Teaching : Required teaching load, if known.
%9. Term : Length of position, if known.  ``1 year + 2 years'' means one
%   year, usually renewed for two more.
%10.Topics : Areas very specifically requested by the department.
%

%(as of 1999) and check it, but I take no responsibility for any mistakes.
